Significant Mathematical Works

Bézier Curve Self-Intersections — Frustrated by the apparent difficulty of a problem I wished to solve, and annoyed by the lack of usable solutions, I decided to come up with my own solution to the problem of finding self-intersections in Bézier curves. I actually wrote a math paper documenting the solution, and currently I am working on a program to test that solution. That has lead me to find errors in my original solution, so I am updating the paper as I find them. As of yet, I have no examples to demonstrate, but once I finish the program, I will put them here. I may even put the paper here as well.

Humans Versus Zombies — For my final project in my Mathematical Modelling class, I chose to model the game of Humans Versus Zombies. Core to this was the investigation of the outcomes of ranged vs. melee battles. Lancaster's Laws only describe ranged vs. ranged battles or melee vs. melee battles. I created the initial battle simulation in Blitz 2D, but then ported it to Python in the interest of better number collection and statistical analysis. The overall model even had potential to become a publishable paper. Below is a snapshot of one frame in my iterative battle model. The blue dots are humans and the red dots are zombies.

Celtic square knots are another mathematical object that I've played with. After I watched this YouTube video, Celtic Knot - 2, I drew some of my own, and then figured out a way to piece them together from tiles, and produced a program to do that. Below is one of the examples.

Group recurrence relations are what I've chosen to call the act of creating a Fibonacci-style sequence with the elements of a (typically finite) group. That is, the sequence begins with any two elements a and b, and the third item in the sequence is the product of a and b: ab. The fourth item is then the product of b and ab, and so forth. These sequences can be graphed, and I have included a few of those graphs below.
