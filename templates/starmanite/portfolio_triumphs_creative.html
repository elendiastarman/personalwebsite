Writing

National Novel Writing Month (NaNoWriMo) — NaNoWriMo is a competition of sorts where the goal is to write at least 50,000 words in the month of November. In 2011, I wrote about 12,000 words, and in 2013, I wrote about 6,000 words. However, in 2012, I managed to write 50,547 words, including two separate days of 10,000 words. I surpassed 50,000 words again in 2014 with 50,049 words. In all four years, I used NaNoWriMo to advance my novel Lumien and the Seven Relics, which is currently around 142,000 words long, but should be more than 180,000 words when finished.

Philosophy paper — For RIT's 5th annual Undergraduate Philosophy Conference, I wrote a paper on the subject of language. It was accepted and I presented it on May 2. It was surprisingly well received.

Blogging — At the time of writing, I have 4 blog posts on my technical blog and 28 blog posts on my personal blog.

Crafting

Turning — I turned a lightsaber handle on a lathe under the supervision and with the teaching of the aforementioned friend.
