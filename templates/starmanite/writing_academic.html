    • A Concept-Web Model of Language

    • The model under consideration is that concepts in a person's mind have labels attached to them and that these concepts are linked in a web of relationships.


    • If a concept is known but a label is missing, then the person who wants to communicate that concept to others must do so by way of related concepts.


    • Concept-labels in the “middle” that are connected to many others are more difficult to alter as doing so would require altering ... the associations between it and other concept-labels, and would ... also require ... some adjustment of related concept-labels.
